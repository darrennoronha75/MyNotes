## Core Concept
While the [[The Normal Distribution]] assumes extreme outliers are rare, real-world data generating processes are often much wilder. Distributions that allocate significant probability to extreme events are called **Heavy-Tailed Distributions**.

## Key Examples
* **The Cauchy Distribution:** A symmetric distribution that looks like a normal curve but has tails so thick that its mean and variance are mathematically undefined. It destroys standard limit laws.
* **The Laplace Distribution:** Symmetrical but with a sharp peak and fatter exponential tails. 
* **Other Variations:** The Exponential, Gamma, and Negative Binomial distributions can also be used to model skewed, heavy-tailed, or strictly positive data where standard Normal assumptions break down.

## Statistical Consequence
When data is generated by a heavy-tailed distribution, standard parametric models (like standard linear regression) can fail catastrophically because they are overly sensitive to the massive outliers these distributions routinely produce.

---
**Connections:**
* When heavy tails are present, standard tools fail, and we must often pivot to robust estimators or non-parametric tests.
