## Core Concept
The Normal (or Gaussian) distribution is the most famous continuous parametric family in statistics, characterized by its symmetric, bell-shaped curve. 

## Parameters
It is strictly defined by exactly two parameters: $N(\mu, \sigma^2)$.
1. **The Mean ($\mu$):** This acts as the location parameter. It completely dictates where the peak of the bell sits on the x-axis. 
2. **The Variance ($\sigma^2$):** This acts as the scale parameter. It dictates the spread of the data. A small variance creates a narrow, spiked bell; a large variance creates a wide, flat bell. The standard deviation ($\sigma$) is simply the square root of the variance.


## Structural Properties
* **Symmetry:** The left side is a perfect mirror image of the right side.
* **Density Drop-off:** As you move further away from the mean ($\mu$) in either direction, the probability density drops off rapidly toward zero, making extreme outliers highly unlikely.
