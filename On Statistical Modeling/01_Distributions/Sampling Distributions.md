# Sampling Distributions

## Definition
A Sampling Distribution is the [[Probability Distribution]] of a given [[Statistic]] (like the sample mean or sample variance) calculated from a random sample. Because a statistic is a random variable, it inherently has its own distribution of possible values across infinite repeated samplings.
## Common Families
When we calculate statistics from normally distributed data, the resulting sampling distributions follow specific mathematical shapes:
* **[[Student's t-Distribution]]:** The sampling distribution used when estimating the mean with an unknown population variance.
* **[[Chi-Squared Distribution]]:** The sampling distribution of estimated variances (sums of squared normal variables).
* **[[F-Distribution]]:** The sampling distribution of the ratio of two variances.

---
**Connections:**
* Sampling distributions act as the primary "Null Distributions" required to perform [[Null Hypothesis Significance Testing (NHST)]].
