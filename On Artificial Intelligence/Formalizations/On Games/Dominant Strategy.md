A strategy is dominant if it provides a higher payoff than any other strategy, regardless of what the other players do.

### Strict vs. Weak Dominance
* **Strict Dominance**: Strategy $s_i$ strictly dominates $s_i'$ if $u_i(s_i, s_{-i}) > u_i(s_i', s_{-i})$ for all $s_{-i}$.
* **Weak Dominance**: Strategy $s_i$ weakly dominates $s_i'$ if $u_i(s_i, s_{-i}) \ge u_i(s_i', s_{-i})$ for all $s_{-i}$ and the inequality is strict for at least one $s_{-i}$.

### Iterative Elimination of Strictly Dominated Strategies (IESDS)
1. Identify a strictly dominated strategy for any player.
2. Remove that strategy (row or column) from the matrix.
3. Repeat until no more strategies can be removed.
* **Note**: The order of elimination does not change the final set of remaining strategies for strict dominance.
