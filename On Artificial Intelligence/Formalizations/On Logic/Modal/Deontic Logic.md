
Deontic logic models "ought" statements. $\Box \phi$ is read as "$\phi$ is **Obligatory**".

### Key Characteristics
* **Axiom T Fails**: We cannot say $\Box \phi \to \phi$ because people break laws. Obligation does not guarantee truth.
* **Axiom D is Required**: $\Box \phi \to \Diamond \phi$.
  - *Translation*: "If $\phi$ is obligatory, then $\phi$ is permitted."
  - *Logical Constraint*: A consistent legal system cannot obligate you to do the impossible (Axiom D ensures there is always at least one "ideal" world accessible).

### Operators
* **Obligatory**: $\Box \phi$
* **Permitted**: $\Diamond \phi$
* **Forbidden**: $\Box \neg \phi$ (equivalent to $\neg \Diamond \phi$)
