
Doxastic logic models the beliefs of agents. $\Box \phi$ is read as **$B_a \phi$** ("Agent $a$ believes $\phi$").

### Epistemic vs. Doxastic Contrast
* **Axiom T Fails**: Unlike knowledge, beliefs can be false. $B_a \phi \to \phi$ is NOT valid.
* **Axioms 4 & 5 Hold**: We assume agents have "Introjective Access" to their own beliefs. If I believe something, I believe I believe it.
* **Axiom D Holds**: We assume rational agents do not believe contradictions. If I believe $\phi$, then $\phi$ is at least possible in my belief state.
