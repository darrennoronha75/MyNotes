
A classic example of a game where individual rationality leads to collective sub-optimality.

### The Matrix
| | Cooperate (C) | Defect (D) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **Cooperate (C)** | $(-1, -1)$ | $(-3, 0)$ |
| **Defect (D)** | $(0, -3)$ | $(-2, -2)$ |

### Analysis
* **Dominant Strategy**: For both players, **Defect (D)** is a strictly dominant strategy because it yields a better payoff regardless of the opponent's choice.
* **Nash Equilibrium**: $(D, D)$ with payoffs $(-2, -2)$.
* **Pareto Optimality**: The NE is not Pareto Optimal, as $(C, C)$ would provide a better payoff for both players $(-1, -1)$.
