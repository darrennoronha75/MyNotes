In game theory, Maximin refers to a strategy where a player attempts to maximize their minimum possible payoff, effectively choosing the "best of the worst-case scenarios".

### Key Concepts
* **Safety First**: It is a risk-averse strategy used when an agent does not know the opponent's strategy or assumes the opponent is out to minimize their payoff.
* **Zero-Sum Games**: In zero-sum games, the Maximin strategy is equivalent to the [[Minimax]] strategy.
* **Security Level**: The Maximin value is the highest value a player can guarantee themselves regardless of the opponent's actions.
