The fundamental existence theorem for non-cooperative games.

> [!abstract] **Theorem Statement**
> Every finite game (finite number of players and finite number of pure strategies) has at least one **Nash Equilibrium** in mixed strategies.

### Implications
* A game might not have a **Pure Strategy Nash Equilibrium** (e.g., Rock-Paper-Scissors).
* However, by allowing players to choose a probability distribution over their strategies (Mixed Strategies), an equilibrium is guaranteed to exist.
