A concept of efficiency in game theory distinct from the Nash Equilibrium.

> [!info] **Definition**
> An outcome is **Pareto Optimal** if there is no other outcome that makes at least one player better off without making any other player worse off.

### Relation to Nash Equilibrium
* A Nash Equilibrium is **not** necessarily Pareto Optimal.
* **Classic Example**: In the [[Prisoner's Dilemma]], the Nash Equilibrium is $(D, D)$ (Mutual Defection), which gives payoffs $(-2, -2)$. However, $(C, C)$ gives $(-1, -1)$, which is better for *both* players. Therefore, the NE is **Pareto Suboptimal**.
